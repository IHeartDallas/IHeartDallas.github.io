Online Tech Education

Over the past two years I set out to enhance my web and software development skills. Even though I am a tech writer and am not new to the software industry, this was and is still is a challenging journey. While I completed a qaulity online program, it only piqued my interest to learn more. I believe many concepts and skills were never addressed or covered. Like many other self-taught developers, you run into many road blocks and finally hit the wall -- Where do I go next!


Since technology drives our world, I strongly believe there is a need for qaulity online tech offerings. Consequently, I would like to share my experience with you in the event that you might have a client that is in this industry. I would be glad if you could share this critique of the industry. Thank you kindly for your time and consideration.

book

Comprehensive

An array of online materials exist. However, there is NOT a particular source that integrates all the necessary content/components together.

videocam

Presentation

Most online learning formats do not include enough practice problems or projects. Consequntly, this diminishes a student's retention of the material.

blur_on

Feed Back

Many online programs do not offer timely responses to questions. This is unusual since we also have so many tools at our disposal such as Zoom, Skype, and Slack.

timer

Antiquated

Tech curriculum quickly becomes outdated. Consequently, students find themselves quilting material together. This creates content gaps.

terrain

Coding Boot Camps

There are quality and reputable coding boot camps. However they are expensive and their locations/times are prohibitive for many.

schoole

College Curriculum

Both two and four-year institutions do not include relevant tech offerings. Those that do, all tend to outsource their offerings via the same organizations.
